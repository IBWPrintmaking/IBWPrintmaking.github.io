Printmaking students dig deep into traditional printmaking techniques while also being encouraged to play with new experimental and innovative approaches.

As a Printmaking student at IBW, you’ll produce innovative artwork in a collaborative, creative learning environment. Learn a broad range of techniques that will allow you to express your ideas with skill. Our studio is equipped with large presses for etching, monoprint, intaglio, linoleum, and lithography. We share a dark room for photo emulsion screen printing to be used for garments and posters and a vinyl printer. Students explore design and typography in sketchbooks, ink, and on computers/ or iPads. Through community events like Live T-Shirt Printing, IBW Art Show, and field trips students are hands-on collaborating with peers and learning about working in the medium today. In our 3rd year AP/Capstone Class, printmaking students can create an AP Art Portfolio to be submitted for possible college credit.

About our Media Printshop:

Do you have a printing job you would like done by our student-run IBW PRINSTHOP? Send us a request at casullivan@pps.net and let’s talk about it. We produce affordable, high-quality screen prints on t-shirts, sweatshirts, and posters using permanent, water-based ink. Proceeds return directly to our program.

Printmaking Teacher:

Katie Sullivan is an artist living in Portland, Oregon with her family. She enjoys learning new and old printmaking combinations in the studio along with drawing and photography. She studied art at Portland State University and earned a teaching certificate from Lewis and Clark. Building up the studio at IBW has been an ongoing passion, and connecting with the printmaking community in Portland. Contact Sullivan at Casullivan@pps.net if you have questions or want to collaborate on a printing project. She would love to hear from you.
